Data Monetization

Telecom Sector Marketing Campaign as a Service Innovation Regulatory Compliance Data/API Monetization
Ooredoo is a fast-paced multi-national corporation offering telecom, cloud, and ICT services in 13 countries with 100m subscribers. Ooredoo Qatar operates in strict adherence to national consumer data privacy laws.
Problem: SMBs sector exhibit low adoption of Bulk SMS service due to lack of customer contact and demographics data, forcing them to outsource marketing campaigns, resulting in missed revenue opportunity for Ooredoo.
Proposition: Unlock a new revenue stream leveraging the company's vast customer data in an anonymized manner to create Marketing Campaigns as a Service platform, allowing SMB segment to launch demographics and location based SMS and WhatsApp marketing campaigns without compromising our customers privacy.

Conceptualized, developed, and launched Marketing Campaign as a Service (MCaaS)—the company's first data/API monetization platform—while successfully navigating complex consumer data privacy laws and regulations.

Business Case: Developed a Business Case based on market analysis, cost of opportunity, regulatory constraints, MVP and sales forecast. Secured executive buy-in and approval to allocate resources. I implemented a pay-per-message model with a premium for advanced targeting capabilities, directly aligning the pricing with the value proposition.
Regulatory and Privacy Clearance: Worked with Regulatory and Legal teams to analyze privacy laws and define way forward options. Presented compliance options' pros and cons to executives demonstrating adherence to all relevant regulations, which culminated in formal approval to proceed, setting a precedent for future data monetization products.
Consent in Touch-Points: Led the effort to embed legal consent language directly into high-traffic customer touchpoints, including all service order forms, digital ordering and electronic signing screens in retail shops. Additionally, I designed and executed a broad-scale communication campaign to establish implied consent for existing subscribers, complemented by updating the data privacy controls within the consumer mobile application to allow for opting out.
Solution Design: Crafted low fidelity wireframes with optimal user flows designed around five steps: 1) selecting campaign audience, 2) writing a message, 3) selecting channel (SMS/WhatsApp), 4) pay for the campaign, and finally 5) launch the campaign. Shared the designs with the UX/UI team to build high fidelity prototypes and accelerate development.
Core Systems APIs: Worked with technical teams to develop integration with SMB customers CRM, consumer data warehouse, Bulk SMS Gateway, Azure Communication Service for WhatsApp integration, and payment gateway. This involved defining clear APIs and data transfer contracts.
