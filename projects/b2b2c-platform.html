B2B2C Telecom Platform

Home Star Service Market Share Innovation B2B2C Collaboration
Ooredoo Qatar is the foremost telecom operator engaging in intense competition with the challenger, Vodafone Qatar, to accelerate their market share penetration rate within one year.
Problem: Vodafone Qatar aggressively targeted Ooredoo's residential subscribers with discounted bundles. Ooredoo lacked a structured channel to leverage its B2B partnerships as a competitive acquisition and retention mechanism for the consumer segment.
Proposition: Create a B2B2C tri-party commercial model — "Home Star Service" — in which Ooredoo partners with large B2B corporate clients (employers, developers, gated communities) to offer exclusive, discounted residential telecom bundles to their employees and residents, driving subscriber acquisition while protecting market share.

The model incentivized operators to prioritize Ooredoo services to achieve marginal profit. Simultaneously, it encouraged demand for these services for the discount and flexible options. This strategic framework led to Ooredoo capturing competitive market share within one year.

Market Share: Worked with the product team to design and execute a market survey to determine the baseline state in consumer (large or large residential complexes, or large residential communities) in a region showing a rapidly eroding market share. The survey identified a highly declining competitor activity showing long-term revenue and a zero-cost customer acquisition channels.
Value Proposition: Created a tri-party commercial model that structurally favored the company's own service over competitors. Corporate partners received a revenue-sharing arrangement and marketing support, while their employees and residents gained access to exclusive Ooredoo bundles at discounted rates, addressing both the acquisition and retention challenge simultaneously.
Conceptual Consulting: Developed comprehensive go-to-market collateral, including co-branded partner playbooks, commercial term sheets, and onboarding kits. Designed a dedicated account management model to maintain and grow each B2B partner relationship, ensuring high activation rates among their employees and residents.
Design Concept: Led design sessions with CRM and BI&A/IT teams to architect and deliver a hybrid split-billing model. The model determined how base charges to the tenant are different from the charges to the tenant's customer, ensuring a differentiated model maintaining competitive advantage and partner profitability.
Consumer App: Partnered with the Consumer App development team to integrate the subscriber service into the consumer mobile application, enabling B2B2C activated subscribers to self-manage their plan, view usage, pay bills, and access technical support — ensuring a premium digital experience for all technical requirements and user acceptance testing.
Digital Billing: Designed the end-to-end billing architecture to handle the tri-party revenue split: Ooredoo retained its service margin, corporate partners received their agreed commission, and end-customers received their discounted statement. Integrated with existing BSS/OSS to ensure no charges to the desktop and no updates to the tenant.
Launch: Successfully launched the platform into production, executing comprehensive sales and training programs for the B2B sales team to manage significant increase of 2% within the first year of operation. The rapid adoption by landlords significantly resulted in a measurable market increase of 2% within the first year of operation.
